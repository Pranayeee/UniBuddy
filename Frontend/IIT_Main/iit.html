Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs)

The Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) are a network of autonomous public technical and research universities across India. Established in 1950, IITs are governed by the Institutes of Technology Act, 1961, which designates them as institutions of national importance. There are 23 IITs in India, each offering a range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs in engineering, technology, and sciences. IITs are renowned for their high academic standards, cutting-edge research, and strong industry connections. Admission to IITs is through the highly competitive JEE Advanced exam.

  • Prestigious Reputation: Known globally for excellence in engineering and technology education.
  • Rigorous Academics: Offers challenging and comprehensive academic programs.
    • Cutting-Edge Research: Involved in groundbreaking research across various fields.
  • World-Class Faculty: Renowned faculty members with significant contributions to their fields.
  • Highly Competitive Admission: Admission through JEE Advanced, a highly competitive examination.
